📊 Synopsis

Defence Holdings PLC is the UK's first listed pure-play defence software platform. Formerly Cassell Capital PLC, it pivoted to defence AI in 2025 and relisted on the LSE Main Market in July 2025.

The business model is a HoldCo structure: ALRT allocates capital, Defence Technologies builds and deploys sovereign-AI products, and an acquisitions pipeline ("Buy-and-Build") adds capability.

Four product domains: AI Agents (mission planning), Drone Warfare (commercial drone aggregation), Information Warfare (deepfake detection, disinformation), and Critical Infrastructure Defence (power grids, telecoms).

The company is pre-revenue from these products but has its first customer in pre-contract pathway (Project Ixian) and has deepened engagement with Gloucestershire Police, Oracle, and allied defence stakeholders. CEO Andrew Roughan (ex-Plexal, strong government/national security network) starts 30 March 2026.

🔴 Execution Risks
CRITICAL — Pre-revenue
Products still in development/pre-contract. No recurring revenue from defence products. Company survives on capital raises and ATM facility.
HIGH — Cash runway
£2.21M cash at Sept 2025. ATM facility provides ongoing dilutive financing. If contracts don't arrive, further dilution is likely. Burn rate vs runway is critical.
HIGH — Dilution risk
2.43 billion shares already in issue. ATM facility and warrant exercises continue to increase share count. EPS accretion is distant.
MED — Contract delays
Government procurement is notoriously slow. Project Ixian has been "in pre-contract pathway" for months. Timelines could slip significantly.
MED — Competition
Palantir, BAE Systems, Thales, QinetiQ all competing for UK/NATO AI defence contracts with far greater resources and established relationships.
🟡 Sector & Macro Risks
MED — Security classification
Working with classified defence systems limits what can be publicly disclosed. Investors may struggle to assess real progress. Information asymmetry is inherent.
MED — Regulatory / security clearance
MOD and government work requires security clearances, vetting, and long procurement timelines. Any issue with management vetting could delay contracts.
MED — Geopolitical dependency
Business case partially built on sustained defence spending increases. Any Ukraine peace deal or NATO budgetary pullback could dampen urgency for new capabilities.
LOW — UK defence budget risk
UK committed to 2.5% GDP defence spend. Strategic Defence Review underway. Direction of travel is firmly pro-software, pro-AI, pro-sovereign capability.
LOW — Technology obsolescence
AI/drone tech evolves fast but ALRT's model (HoldCo acquirer + deployer) means it can pivot to new technologies. Not locked into single-product risk.
